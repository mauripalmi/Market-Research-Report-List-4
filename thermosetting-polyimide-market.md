<p><p>Thermosetting polyimide is used in various market types including extrusion molding, hot compression molding, direct forming, isotactic pressing, and other markets. Extrusion molding involves forcing the polyimide through a die to create a specific shape. Hot compression molding uses heat and pressure to form the polyimide into a desired shape. Direct forming involves shaping the polyimide directly into the desired form. Isostatic pressing applies pressure from all directions to mold the polyimide. Other markets may involve unique processes or applications for thermosetting polyimide.</p></p>
