<p><p>Fruit concentrates are typically classified into two main types: liquid concentrate and powder concentrate. Liquid concentrate is a thick and viscous form of concentrate that retains the natural fruit flavors and nutrients, making it suitable for adding to beverages and foods. On the other hand, powder concentrate is a dehydrated form of concentrate that is easier to store and transport. It is typically reconstituted with water before use, making it convenient for manufacturers and consumers alike. Both types offer convenience and versatility in various applications.</p></p>
