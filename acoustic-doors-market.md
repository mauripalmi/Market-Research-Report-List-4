<p><p>Acoustic doors are classified into two main types based on their structure: single and double doors. Single doors are made up of a single panel, providing a basic level of sound insulation. On the other hand, double doors consist of two panels with an air gap between them, offering higher acoustic performance. Double doors are typically used in areas where greater soundproofing is required, such as recording studios, theaters, and conference rooms. Both single and double doors play a crucial role in controlling noise transmission and creating soundproof environments.</p></p>
