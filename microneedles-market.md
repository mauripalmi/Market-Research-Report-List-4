There are two main types of microneedle technologies: Hollow Microneedle Technology and Solid Microneedle Technology. </p><p>Hollow Microneedles have channels through which drugs or other substances can be delivered directly into the skin. This technology is advantageous for delivering larger molecules or vaccines. </p><p>Solid Microneedles do not have channels and work by physically puncturing the skin to create pathways for drug delivery. This technology is often used for simpler drug delivery applications.</p></p>
