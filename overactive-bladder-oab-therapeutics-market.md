<p><p>Overactive Bladder (OAB) Therapeutics market includes two main types of medications - Anticholinergic Agents and Beta-3 Adrenoreceptor Agonists. Anticholinergic Agents work by blocking certain nerve impulses that cause the bladder muscles to contract uncontrollably, helping to reduce the symptoms of OAB. On the other hand, Beta-3 Adrenoreceptor Agonists work by relaxing the bladder muscles to improve bladder control. Both types of medications are commonly used in the treatment of OAB to help manage the symptoms of urgency, frequency, and incontinence.</p></p>
