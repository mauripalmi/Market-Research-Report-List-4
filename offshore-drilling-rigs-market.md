<p><p>Offshore drilling rigs are classified into three main types based on their design and capabilities. Jackup rigs are commonly used in shallow waters and are self-elevating platforms. Semi-submersibles are floating platforms that use underwater pontoons for stability in rough waters. Drillships are vessels equipped with drilling equipment and can operate in deep waters. Each type of rig serves a specific purpose in the offshore drilling industry, catering to different depth requirements and environmental conditions.</p></p>
