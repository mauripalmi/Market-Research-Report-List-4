<p><p>The automotive brake system consists of various components such as the brake booster, master cylinder, drum brake, and disc brake. The brake booster helps enhance the force applied to the brake pedal, while the master cylinder converts the force into hydraulic pressure. Drum brakes and disc brakes are two types of braking systems used in vehicles, with drum brakes applying pressure to the inner surface of a rotating drum, and disc brakes using calipers to squeeze pads against a disc to create friction and slow down the vehicle.</p></p>
