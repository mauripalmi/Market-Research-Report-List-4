<p><p>Asthma immunotherapies include subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT) and sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT). SCIT involves injecting allergens under the skin to build tolerance and reduce asthma symptoms. SLIT, on the other hand, involves placing allergens under the tongue to achieve similar results. Both types of immunotherapy are aimed at desensitizing the immune system to specific allergens, reducing the severity and frequency of asthma attacks. These treatments offer a long-term solution for asthma management by addressing the underlying cause of the condition.</p></p>
