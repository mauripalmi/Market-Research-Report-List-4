<p><p>Vacant gelatin capsules come in different types based on their functionality. Gastric soluble capsules dissolve quickly in the stomach, making them ideal for immediate release of medications. On the other hand, enteric capsules have a delayed release mechanism, designed to withstand stomach acid and dissolve in the intestines. This allows for targeted drug delivery and reduces the risk of stomach irritation. Both types cater to different needs in the pharmaceutical industry, offering a variety of options for formulating and delivering medications.</p></p>
