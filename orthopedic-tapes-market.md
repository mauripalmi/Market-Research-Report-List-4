<p><p>Orthopedic tapes can be classified into two main types: rigid and flexible. Rigid orthopedic tapes are designed to provide strong support and immobilization to the affected area, commonly used in cases of fractures or severe injuries. On the other hand, flexible orthopedic tapes are more pliable and allow for greater movement, making them suitable for less severe injuries or for use during physical activity. Both types of orthopedic tapes serve the purpose of providing support, stability, and protection to injured joints, muscles, or ligaments.</p></p>
